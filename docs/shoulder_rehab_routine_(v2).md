# Shoulder Rehab Routine

## General Structure

- Frequency: 4x/week
- Alternate days when possible: 1 day on, 1 day off
- Most exercises:
  - 10 repetitions
  - 2 sets
- Pain rule:
  - Stop at the pain level
  - Do not push through sharp pain
- Main cue throughout:
  - Keep the shoulder down
  - Avoid shrugging
  - Move slowly and with control

---

# Recommended Order

1. Neck / posture activation
2. Shoulder and upper-back mobility
3. Scapular control drills
4. Resistance-band strengthening
5. Recovery: cold after exercises if irritated

---

# Exercise 1 — Band-Resisted Chin Tuck / Neck Retraction

## Setup

- Stand upright with good posture.
- Resistance band goes behind the back of the head.
- Arms are extended forward holding the band.

## Movement

- Gently move the head backward against the resistance.
- Motion is subtle — like making a “double chin.”
- Do not tilt the head upward.
- Hold for a couple of seconds.
- Return slowly to neutral.

## Important Cues

- Keep shoulders relaxed.
- Avoid shrugging.
- Keep the movement small and controlled.

## Volume

- 10 reps
- 2 sets

---

# Exercise 2 — Foam Roller Wall Slide with Resistance Band

## Setup

- Sit facing a wall.
- Place a long foam roller horizontally against the wall.
- Put a resistance band around both wrists.
- Elbows are bent in front of you.
- Hands point upward.
- Keep slight outward tension on the band.

## Movement

- Slide the foam roller upward along the wall.
- Keep pushing gently outward into the resistance band.
- Go up until the pain limit.
- Slide back down slowly.

## Important Cues

- Keep shoulders down.
- Do not shrug.
- Keep movement smooth.
- Stop at pain level.

## Volume

- 10 reps
- 2 sets

---

# Exercise 3 — Foam Roller Prayer Stretch / Lat Stretch

## Setup

- Stand facing a high table or bed.
- Place the long foam roller on the table/bed.
- Put both hands or forearms on the roller.
- Hinge forward at the hips.
- Keep back long and neutral.

## Movement

- Roll arms forward while moving hips backward.
- Let chest lower gently toward the floor.
- Feel stretch through shoulders, lats, and upper back.
- Return slightly, then repeat.

## Important Cues

- Keep the back long.
- Do not collapse into the shoulder.
- Do not force the stretch.
- Stop at pain level.

## Volume

- 8–10 slow reps
- 1–2 sets
- Optional: pause 2–3 seconds at the end of each rep

---

# Exercise 4 — Ball-on-Wall Stability Drill

## Setup

- Use a small ball against the wall.
- Place hand or forearm on the ball.
- Keep arm in a comfortable, non-painful height.

## Movement

Perform controlled movements:

1. Up and down
2. Side to side
3. Small controlled circles or directional movements

## Important Cues

- Keep shoulder down.
- Do not raise arm too high.
- Keep movements small and controlled.
- Avoid painful range.

## Volume

- 10 reps each direction
- 2 sets

---

# Exercise 5 — Quadruped Rotation / Thread-the-Needle Variation

## Setup

- Start on hands and knees.
- Hands under shoulders.
- Knees under hips.

## Movement

1. Reach one arm forward.
2. Rotate outward/open to the side.
3. Reach underneath torso toward the opposite side.
4. Return to start position.

## Important Cues

- Move slowly.
- Do not collapse downward.
- Keep the motion controlled.
- Stay within comfortable range.

## Volume

- 10 reps each side
- 2 sets

---

# Exercise 6 — Half Squat + Resistance Band Shoulder Control

## Setup

- Anchor resistance band low.
- Stand in a slight half squat.
- Keep back straight.
- Hold the band with arms extended forward.

## Movement

- Pull with controlled movement.
- Elbows move down/back.
- Arms move outward and forward under control.
- Maintain tension throughout.

## Important Cues

- Do not raise shoulders.
- Keep shoulder blades engaged.
- Avoid upper trap compensation.

## Volume

- 10 reps
- 2 sets

---

# Exercise 7 — Single-Arm Banded Shoulder Circle

## Setup

- Stand on one end of a long resistance band.
- Hold the other end with one hand.
- Arm is slightly below shoulder height, in the scapular plane.
- Forearm is closer to the chest, not fully out to the side.

## Movement

- Raise the arm forward/outward like holding a flag.
- Make controlled circular movements.
- Keep shoulder down.
- Perform circles in both directions.

## Important Cues

- Do not shrug.
- Do not turn this into a deltoid workout.
- Keep the arm slightly below shoulder height.
- Stop at pain level.

## Volume

- 10 circles one direction
- 10 circles other direction
- 2 sets

---

# Exercise 8 — Single-Arm Banded “Wing Spread”

## Setup

- Same setup as Exercise 7.
- Stand on the band.
- Hold the band with one hand.
- Arm begins slightly forward and below shoulder height.

## Movement

- Reach forward slightly with the shoulder.
- Then open the arm outward, like spreading a wing.
- Return slowly to the starting position.

## Important Cues

- Keep shoulder down.
- Keep movement controlled.
- Avoid pinching or sharp pain.
- Stop at pain level.

## Volume

- 10 reps
- 2 sets

---

# Exercise 9 — Banded Forward Serratus Reach

## Setup

- Stand upright.
- Elbows bent at your sides.
- Resistance band around wrists.
- Wrists stay under shoulder line.
- Keep outward tension on the band.

## Movement

- Reach both arms forward.
- Keep the band tense.
- Hold for about 2 seconds.
- Return slowly to the starting position.

## Important Cues

- Do not shrug.
- Keep shoulders down.
- Keep band tension the whole time.
- Move smoothly.

## Volume

- 10 reps
- 2 sets

---

# Exercise 10 — Prone Dumbbell Shoulder Sequence

## Setup

- Lie face down on a bench/table.
- Support forehead to avoid neck strain.
- Arms begin hanging downward.
- Use very light weight.

## Part A — Side Raises

- Lift arms sideways.
- Do not raise too high.
- Lower slowly.

## Part B — Forward/Backward Movement

- Move arms forward and backward under control.

## Part C — Small Circles

- Make small circles:
  - 5 in one direction
  - 5 in the other direction

## Important Cues

- Use low weight.
- Avoid shrugging.
- Keep movement slow and controlled.

## Volume

- 10 reps each movement
- 2 sets

# Daily Stretches — Morning and Before Bed

## Neck Stretch 1

- 2 reps each side
- One hand holds top of head.
- Tilt head toward that shoulder.
- Opposite arm hangs down with palm facing forward.
- Push opposite fingertips gently toward the floor.
- Hold around 5 seconds.
- Breathe and gently move further into range.

## Neck Stretch 2

- 2 reps each side
- One hand holds back of head.
- Head faces diagonally down toward the shoulder.
- Opposite arm moves gently backward.
- Hold around 5 seconds.
- Breathe and gently move further into range.

## Shoulder Shrugs

- Shrug shoulders backward for 10 seconds.
- Shrug shoulders forward for 10 seconds.

## Side-to-Side Head Rotation

- Start looking sideways and slightly up.
- Rotate toward the other side while looking down.
- Finish looking sideways and slightly up on the other side.
- Do not force the upward position.

# Heat / Cold

## Morning

- Use warm water or heat on the shoulder for a few minutes.

## After Exercises

- Use cold pack or cold water for a few minutes if irritated.
- Around 2 minutes of cold water was mentioned.
